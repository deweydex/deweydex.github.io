Week of Sept. 15: Introduction & First Task

Getting Started

Please fill out the "Getting to Know You as a Human Being Form". Note, the more you type, the better I can understand your situation and tailor assignments and feedback to your specific background, circumstances and goals.

Introductory Task

On a new word doc please answer the following questions and go through the process below:

  1. What are three surprising or useful facts that you carry around with you? If you can't think of any how would you go about finding some? What sites or tools would you use?
  2. Where did you learn these facts? If you wanted to verify them, how would you?

Task: Try to verify the facts that you have specified. What are your sources, how do you know that that source is credible or "good"? Do you find any conflicting sources?

Reflection (150-250 words): After you have answered those questions, please write a reflection on what was easy or difficult about these questions and this task: what was unexpected? How did you verify your source? If you had to challenge or critique how you went about answering those questions, what would you say?

Week of Oct. 6: The Humble Banana Project Introduction

Framing Project: The Humble Banana

Dear students,

In this class, we will be discussing framing. More importantly, we will be discussing a highly contentious topic with broad and deep societal and historical significance: Bananas. This topic has too often been viewed as banal, mundane, quotidian, and other less fancy words for "everyday."

Together, we will approach this topic from four different frames, in an attempt to better understand not just the role of the humble banana in our society, but also how the way we frame facts changes their power, their relevance, and their scope. We will discuss argument, form, and tone, as well as investigating both what sources are easily accessible and what frames those resources contain within them.

Choose ONE of the following frames:

  1. Big Banana: Economic and corporate perspective
  2. Bananas For All: Access, equity, and social justice perspective
  3. Badnana: Environmental and ethical concerns perspective
  4. BAN-Bananas: Critical analysis and activism perspective

Your Tasks for Today:

  1. Take out a laptop and sign in
  2. Have a look at the four frames, pick a frame and let me know what you are interested in doing so we have an equal number for each one
  3. Start working on research and writing!
