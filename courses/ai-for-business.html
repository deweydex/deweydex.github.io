Introduction

Over the course of this year, we will explore a series of concepts, tools and best practices regarding three different forms of what generally is called "Artificial Intelligence" but we will refer to as three distinct machine learning applications. This will mean dispelling some myths, uncovering and addressing some biases, and developing both facility and literacy surrounding how ML systems work, how they are sold to businesses, and how they are depicted in media. Hopefully this will enable us to better sort fact from fiction, use these tools effectively, ethically, and safely, and avoid both typical pitfalls and the snake-oil salesmen of today.

This is where our class summaries, reflection questions and other assignments will be posted, and you are welcome to email me with any questions or if you would like feedback on your assignments beyond what we discuss in class. After our initial explorations and building a solid digital foundation, some of the material of this course can be found on LinkedIn Learning.

Week 3: Elaboration on AI and ML Tools

Before delving into the intricacies of how Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) operate, it's essential to explore various tools that we interact with daily. Many applications we use unknowingly leverage AI and ML technologies.

When examining these tools, we can adopt different perspectives: understanding the appropriate context for their usage, identifying potential challenges and risks, and ensuring ethical and safe engagement with these tools.

Weeks 6-9: Using AI Tools for LinkedIn Profiles

Plan for Today

  • Go to LinkedIn and Google Gemini and log in

  • Include the 3 features we discussed: Context, Format, and Tone

Three Parts of a Prompt

  • Context: What is your why and for whom? Where will this be shown?
  • Format: How should your response be structured?
  • Tone: How do you want your response to sound?
